Asphalt Shingle Roof Replacement in San Francisco

San Francisco homeowners frequently choose asphalt shingle roofing systems because they offer durability, affordability, and excellent weather resistance for the Bay Area climate. Whether a roof has reached the end of its life or has sustained storm damage, a professional asphalt shingle roof replacement ensures the home remains protected from moisture, wind, and structural deterioration.

At ROOF EXPRESS, replacing aging roofs with high-performance asphalt shingles is one of the most common projects completed across San Francisco neighborhoods. From Sunset District homes exposed to coastal fog to properties in Noe Valley and Pacific Heights facing wind-driven rain, a properly installed asphalt roof system can last 25–35 years when built with the right materials and installation methods.

Why Asphalt Shingle Roofs Are Popular in San Francisco

Asphalt shingles are the most widely installed roofing material in the United States, and they are especially suitable for San Francisco’s climate. Their layered composition provides strong protection against wind, moisture, and UV exposure while maintaining a clean architectural appearance that complements many Bay Area homes.

Modern architectural shingles such as GAF Timberline HDZ, CertainTeed Landmark PRO, and Owens Corning Duration are designed with advanced adhesive strips and algae-resistant granules. These features improve wind resistance and help maintain the roof’s color and durability over time.

For many San Francisco homeowners, asphalt shingles provide the perfect balance between performance, longevity, and cost efficiency.

The Asphalt Shingle Roof Replacement Process

A professional asphalt shingle roof replacement follows a structured installation process to ensure long-term performance and compliance with California building codes.

Roof Inspection and Permit Process

The project typically begins with a detailed inspection to evaluate the roof structure, ventilation, and decking condition. In San Francisco, most full roof replacements require a permit to ensure that the work meets safety and structural standards.

Tear-Off of the Existing Roof

Old roofing materials are carefully removed down to the roof deck. This step is critical because it allows contractors to inspect the underlying plywood or plank decking and replace any damaged sections.

Roof Deck Preparation

Once exposed, the roof deck is reinforced where necessary. New plywood or OSB sheathing may be installed to create a stable surface for the new roofing system.

Waterproof Underlayment Installation

High-quality underlayment is applied to protect the structure from water infiltration. Many professional installations include synthetic underlayment and ice & water barrier membranes in vulnerable areas such as valleys, eaves, and around penetrations.

Shingle Installation

Architectural asphalt shingles are installed in overlapping layers to create a waterproof barrier. Starter shingles, ridge caps, and proper nailing patterns are essential to ensure wind resistance and long-term durability.

Ventilation and Final Inspection

Proper attic ventilation is crucial for maintaining roof health. Ridge vents, intake vents, or other ventilation solutions help regulate temperature and prevent moisture buildup inside the attic.

Benefits of Installing a New Asphalt Shingle Roof

A professionally installed asphalt shingle roof offers several important advantages for homeowners in San Francisco.

First, it improves weather resistance, protecting the home from rain, fog, and coastal wind conditions common throughout the Bay Area.

Second, it increases property value and curb appeal. New architectural shingles provide a clean and modern appearance that enhances the overall look of the home.

Third, modern asphalt shingles are engineered for energy efficiency and durability, helping reduce long-term maintenance costs.

When Should a San Francisco Homeowner Replace Their Roof?

Many roofs in San Francisco begin showing signs of aging after 20–30 years. Homeowners should consider replacement if they notice:

• Curling or missing shingles

• Granule loss or dark streaks

• Persistent roof leaks

• Sagging or damaged decking

• Storm or wind damage

Addressing these issues early can prevent costly structural damage to the home.

Choosing the Right Roofing Contractor in San Francisco

Roof replacement is a major investment, so selecting a qualified contractor is essential. Homeowners should work with licensed, insured roofing professionals who follow California building codes and use high-quality materials from reputable manufacturers.

A trusted roofing company will also provide clear project timelines, warranty coverage, and transparent communication throughout the installation process.
