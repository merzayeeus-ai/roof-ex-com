Roof Replacement in San Francisco & the Bay Area A Homeowner’s Guide by Roof Express

Replacing a roof is one of the smartest upgrades you can make for your home—when it’s done at the right time and with the right system. A quality roof replacement protects your property from Bay Area wind-driven rain, improves energy efficiency, boosts curb appeal, and can prevent costly structural repairs later.

At Roof Express, we install high-performance roofing systems for homes and buildings across San Francisco and the Bay Area, with a focus on clean workmanship, clear communication, and long-lasting results.

How to Know It’s Time for a Roof Replacement

Some roofs need repair—others are telling you it’s time to replace. Here are the most common signs:

Repeated leaks in multiple areas

Shingles curling, cracking, or missing

Granules washing into gutters (asphalt shingle wear)

Soft spots when walking the roof (possible wood rot)

Sagging roof lines or uneven areas

Flashing failures around chimneys/skylights that keep returning

Roof age near or beyond expected lifespan

Daylight visible from attic gaps or compromised decking

If your roof is leaking and also showing age-related wear, replacement often becomes more cost-effective than repeated patching.

Flat or low-slope roofs require a system designed for water resistance and drainage.

Modified bitumen / torch-down systems

Benefits: Strong waterproofing, durable seams, excellent for coastal weather

What’s Included in a Professional Roof Replacement

A real roof replacement is more than “new shingles.” A high-quality system includes:

Tear-off of old roofing (when needed)

Deck inspection and replacement of damaged plywood/sheathing

Underlayment installation (water barrier layer)

Ice & water protection in critical areas (as appropriate)

New flashing system (chimney, skylight, walls, edges, valleys)

Proper ventilation (helps prevent moisture and heat buildup)

Drip edge and roof edge protection

New roof covering (shingles or flat-roof system)

Cleanup and final walkthrough

At Roof Express, we focus heavily on flashing and detail work, because that’s where most leaks start.

How Long Does a Roof Replacement Take?

Most residential roof replacements take 1–3 days, depending on:

roof size and pitch

number of layers to remove

wood repairs needed

weather and access

Flat-roof projects can vary based on system type and drain/edge details.

Roof Replacement Cost in San Francisco: What Affects Price?

Roof replacement pricing depends on several factors:

roof size (square footage / “squares”)

roof pitch and complexity

number of penetrations (skylights, vents, chimneys)

wood replacement needs

material selection and warranty level

access and parking limitations (common in SF)

The best way to get an accurate price is an on-site inspection—especially if there are leak or decking concerns.

Choosing the Right Roofing Contractor

When comparing roof replacement bids, look for:

Clear scope (tear-off, underlayment, flashing, ventilation, cleanup)

Photos and documentation of existing issues

Warranty details (materials + workmanship)

Local experience with SF weather and building conditions

Professional communication and clean job-site standards
