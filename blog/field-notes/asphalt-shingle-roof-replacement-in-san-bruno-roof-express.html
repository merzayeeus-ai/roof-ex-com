The San Bruno Roofing Challenge: Climate Matters Living in San Bruno means enjoying beautiful coastal weather, but it also means your home is subjected to a unique set of environmental stressors.

Coastal Moisture & Fog: The heavy morning fog rolling over the hills introduces constant moisture, which can accelerate wear and tear on inferior roofing materials.

Intense UV Exposure: During the summer, the relentless California sun beats down on your roof, breaking down the asphalt and driving up indoor temperatures.

High Winds: The geography of the Peninsula often creates wind tunnels, making wind uplift a serious threat to loose or aging shingles. To combat these local elements, a standard, run-of-the-mill roof simply will not cut it. You need a roofing system specifically engineered for the California climate.

The Solution: CertainTeed Landmark® PRO Solaris® Shingles For this San Bruno project, the Roof Express team installed **CertainTeed Landmark PRO Solaris** asphalt shingles. As a top-rated roofing contractor, we have hands-on experience with nearly every material on the market, and this specific shingle line stands out as a premier choice for Bay Area homeowners.

Here is a deep dive into why we consistently recommend this product:

1. Superior Energy Efficiency (Title 24 Compliant) California’s Title 24 energy standards are strict, and for good reason. The Solaris line features advanced **cool roof technology**. These shingles are manufactured with specially designed reflective granules that bounce solar energy back into the atmosphere rather than absorbing it into your attic. The Result: A significantly cooler home during the summer, less strain on your HVAC system, and noticeably lower monthly energy bills.

2. Maximum Durability and Weather Defense The Landmark PRO series is built with a dual-layer, high-performance design that offers an extra-thick layer of weather protection.

Impact Resistance:They are highly resistant to impact from falling branches or heavy hail. Wind Resistance: Engineered to stay put, these shingles carry an impressive wind resistance rating of up to 110 mph (and up to 130 mph with special installation upgrades).

Fire Safety: They boast an Underwriters Laboratories (UL) Class A fire resistance rating, giving homeowners ultimate peace of mind. 3. Unmatched Architectural Curb Appeal A new roof should be as beautiful as it is functional. The "Max Def" (Maximum Definition) color palette of the Landmark PRO series offers a richer mixture of surface granules. This creates a deeply dimensional, wood-shake look that instantly elevates the visual aesthetic and resale value of the property.

The Roof Express Installation Process: Step-by-Step A premium shingle can only perform perfectly if it is installed perfectly. Our team at Roof Express follows a strict, quality-controlled process to ensure every project crosses the finish line with zero defects. Here is how we handled this San Bruno completion:

Phase 1: Comprehensive Inspection and Protection Before a single shingle is removed, we protect the property. We strategically place tarps over landscaping, driveways, and patios to catch debris. We also conduct a final structural check to prepare for the tear-off.

Phase 2: The Tear-Off We completely remove the old, failing roof down to the bare wooden decking. **[Insert Internal Link: Learn more about our tear-off process here]**. We never recommend "roof-overs" (installing new shingles over old ones), as this hides underlying rot and voids manufacturer warranties.

Phase 3: Deck Prep and Underlayment Once the decking is exposed, we inspect every square inch for wood rot or water damage, replacing any compromised boards. Next, we install a high-quality, synthetic underlayment. This acts as a secondary, waterproof barrier between the wood deck and the new shingles. We also install heavy-duty ice and water shields in the roof valleys and around penetrations (like vents and chimneys).

Phase 4: Precision Shingle Installation This is where the Roof Express crew truly shines. Using chalk lines for laser-straight rows, we install the CertainTeed Landmark PRO Solaris shingles using a specialized nailing pattern. Every nail is driven flush and positioned perfectly on the nail line to ensure maximum wind resistance.

Phase 5: Ventilation and Flashing A roof must breathe to last. We install brand-new, customized flashing around all roof penetrations to prevent leaks. We also upgrade the attic ventilation systems (ridge vents and soffit vents) to ensure proper airflow, which prevents moisture buildup and prolongs the life of the new shingles.

Phase 6: The Final Cleanup We believe we should leave your property looking better than we found it. We use heavy-duty magnetic sweepers across your entire yard and driveway to pick up any stray nails, ensuring a safe environment for your family and pets.
