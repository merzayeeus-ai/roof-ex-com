Two-Day Flat Roof Replacement in San Francisco: Permit-Approved Torch-Down Roofing Installation by ROOF EXPRESS

San Francisco flat roof replacement projects require careful planning, proper permits, and experienced installation crews to ensure long-term waterproof protection. In this recent project, the professional team at ROOF EXPRESS completed a full flat roof replacement in San Francisco within just two days, starting with permit approval and finishing with a durable modified bitumen torch-down roofing system. This project highlights how efficient planning, skilled workmanship, and high-quality roofing materials can deliver a long-lasting roofing system that protects Bay Area homes from moisture intrusion, wind exposure, and heavy seasonal rain.

Flat Roof Replacement in San Francisco with Permit Approval

Before beginning any roofing work, the project was reviewed and approved through the San Francisco Department of Building Inspection to ensure full compliance with local building codes and safety standards. Roofing permits are essential for protecting homeowners and ensuring that roof replacement projects meet city regulations. Once the permit was issued, the ROOF EXPRESS crew scheduled the installation and prepared all necessary materials including modified bitumen membranes, base sheets, cap sheets, flashing components, and drainage accessories.

Preparing the project in advance allowed the installation team to complete the roof efficiently while maintaining strict safety and quality standards.

Day One: Roof Tear-Off and Structural Deck Inspection

On the first day of the project, the crew removed the existing flat roofing layers to expose the roof decking and inspect the structural condition of the roof. Many older flat roofs in San Francisco contain multiple layers of roofing materials that may trap moisture over time, making a complete tear-off necessary. After removing the old roofing system, the team carefully inspected the plywood decking and replaced any damaged or weakened sections to create a strong foundation for the new roofing system.

Once the deck was secure and properly prepared, the crew installed a professional base layer designed to enhance adhesion and provide additional moisture protection.

Day Two: Modified Bitumen Torch-Down Roofing Installation

On the second day, the installation team applied a multi-layer modified bitumen torch-down roofing system, which is one of the most reliable flat roofing solutions used throughout San Francisco. This roofing method uses heat-fused layers that bond together to create a seamless waterproof membrane. The system included a reinforced base sheet followed by a durable cap sheet designed to resist UV exposure, water ponding, and temperature changes common in coastal climates.

Flashings around vents, roof penetrations, skylights, and roof edges were carefully sealed to prevent leaks and improve long-term durability. Proper drainage flow was also verified to prevent water pooling, which is a common issue on flat roofs.

Final Roof Inspection and Project Completion

After installation, the ROOF EXPRESS team performed a final inspection to confirm that the roofing system met both city permit requirements and professional roofing standards. The newly installed flat roof now provides improved waterproof protection, enhanced durability, and a clean modern appearance suitable for San Francisco residential properties. Completing a full flat roof replacement in just two days demonstrates the efficiency and experience of a professional roofing contractor while maintaining full compliance with city regulations and industry best practices.

Why Modified Bitumen Roofing Is Ideal for San Francisco Flat Roofs

Modified bitumen roofing systems are widely used for flat and low-slope roofs because they provide exceptional durability, flexibility, and waterproof performance. These roofing systems can last 20 years or more when installed correctly and maintained regularly. Their layered structure provides superior resistance to moisture, UV rays, and thermal expansion, making them ideal for the fluctuating climate conditions found in the San Francisco Bay Area.
