Torch-Down Roofing in San Mateo (2026 Guide) Commercial Flat Roof Installation & Replacement

Torch-down roofing is one of the most reliable systems for commercial flat roofs in San Mateo. Designed for durability and waterproof performance, torch-down (modified bitumen) systems provide strong protection against coastal moisture, heavy rain, and sun exposure.

For warehouses, office buildings, retail spaces, and multi-unit properties, torch-down roofing delivers long-term performance when installed correctly.

What Is Torch-Down Roofing?

4

Torch-down roofing is a modified bitumen membrane system applied with heat. The membrane is fused to the roof surface using a propane torch, creating a fully sealed, waterproof layer.

It is commonly installed in a 2-ply system:

1️⃣ Base sheet

2️⃣ Cap sheet (heat-welded surface layer)

This layered system provides superior waterproofing and durability.

Why Torch-Down Roofing Is Ideal for San Mateo

Commercial buildings in San Mateo experience:

🌧 Heavy seasonal rain

🌫 Coastal moisture exposure

☀️ UV heat stress

💨 Wind uplift pressure

Torch-down systems perform well in these conditions due to their strong seam bonding and multi-layer design.

Benefits of Torch-Down Roofing

✔ Excellent waterproof protection

✔ Durable multi-layer system

✔ Strong resistance to punctures

✔ Long lifespan (20+ years)

✔ Suitable for low-slope roofs

✔ Easy repair if needed

When properly installed, torch-down roofing provides dependable flat roof performance.

Torch-Down Roofing Installation Process 1️⃣ Roof Inspection

Assessment of existing membrane, decking, and insulation.

2️⃣ Surface Preparation

Removal of old roofing materials if necessary and inspection of roof deck.

3️⃣ Base Sheet Installation

The base layer is installed and secured properly.

4️⃣ Cap Sheet Torch Application

The cap sheet is heat-welded to create sealed seams and waterproof protection.

5️⃣ Flashing & Drainage Sealing

Special attention is given to:

Roof penetrations

Parapet walls

Drains and scuppers

HVAC curbs

6️⃣ Final Inspection

Seams and edges are checked for complete adhesion.

Signs Your Torch-Down Roof Needs Repair

Ponding water

Cracked or blistered membrane

Open seams

Leaks near penetrations

Aging roof (20+ years)

Early repair prevents widespread damage.

How Long Does Torch-Down Roofing Last?

20–25+ years lifespan

Regular inspections extend durability

Proper drainage is essential for maximum lifespan.
