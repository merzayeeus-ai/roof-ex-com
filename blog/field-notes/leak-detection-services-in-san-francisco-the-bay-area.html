What Is Roof Leak Detection?

Roof leak detection is the process of locating where water is entering your roofing system—often through seams, flashings, penetrations, skylights, vents, or hidden damage beneath the surface. In many cases, the roof may look “fine” from the street, but still be letting water in.

A good leak detection inspection looks beyond the obvious and checks:

Roofing materials (shingles, flat roof membranes, modified bitumen, etc.)

Flashing systems (chimney, wall, skylight, valley, and edge)

Roof penetrations (pipes, vents, HVAC curbs)

Drainage and ponding water issues on flat roofs

Attic ventilation and moisture patterns

Underlayment and sheathing (when accessible)

Signs You May Have a Roof Leak

If you notice any of these, it’s time to schedule a leak detection inspection:

Brown water stains on ceiling or walls

Bubbling paint or peeling drywall

Musty odors (especially after rain)

Dripping in the attic or damp insulation

Mold growth near vents, skylights, or corners

Warped baseboards or wet flooring near exterior walls

Missing, lifted, or cracked shingles

Ponding water on flat roof areas 48+ hours after rain

In San Francisco’s coastal climate, wind-driven rain can push water into tiny openings—so even small roof issues can leak during storms.

Common Roof Leak Causes in the Bay Area 1) Flashing failures

Most leaks come from flashing, not the main roofing field. Flashing can loosen over time, corrode, crack, or separate at joints—especially around chimneys, skylights, parapet walls, and valleys.

2) Skylight and vent penetrations

Sealant around skylights and vents doesn’t last forever. Gaskets dry out, fasteners loosen, and older skylights often leak at the curb or frame.

3) Aging or damaged roofing materials

Shingles can crack, slide, or lose granules. Flat roofs can develop seam separation, blisters, punctures, or membrane shrinkage.

4) Poor drainage on flat roofs

Clogged drains, low spots, or negative slope leads to ponding water, which increases leak risk and can shorten roof life.

5) Improper roof repairs

Quick patches with roofing cement or random caulking often fail—especially when the underlying issue is flashing design, movement, or water traveling under the system.

Shingle Roof Leak Detection

Should You Patch a Leak Yourself?

If water is actively pouring in, a temporary step can help reduce damage:

Place a bucket and protect flooring

If safe, tarp the area (only if you can do it safely)

Avoid climbing on steep or wet roofs

But for most leaks, DIY caulking is a short-lived fix. The risk is spending money and time on a patch that doesn’t address the entry point—then dealing with hidden rot and mold later.

What Happens If You Ignore a Roof Leak?

Roof leaks are rarely “small” for long. Delaying repairs can lead to:

rotted plywood and framing

mold and indoor air quality issues

ruined insulation (higher energy costs)

damaged drywall and electrical hazards

shortened roof lifespan and higher replacement cost

Leak detection early is usually the most cost-effective move.

Frequently Asked Questions How long does leak detection take?

Most inspections take 30–90 minutes, depending on roof size, access, and complexity.

Can you find a leak if it’s not raining?

Often yes. Interior indicators, roof detail checks, and common failure points can reveal the source. Some leaks are intermittent, so we document conditions and recommend the most likely fixes.

Is a leak always a sign I need a new roof?

Not always. Many leaks are repairable—especially if caught early. If the roof is near end-of-life or has widespread issues, we’ll tell you honestly.
