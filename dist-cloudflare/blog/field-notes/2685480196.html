When replacing a roof, most homeowners focus on shingles. But what truly supports and protects your home is the roof decking—typically made from plywood or OSB (oriented strand board).

If the decking is weak, rotted, or improperly installed, even the best roofing materials will fail.

4

Roof decking (also called roof sheathing) is the wooden structural layer attached to roof rafters or trusses. It forms the solid surface where underlayment and shingles are installed.

Common decking materials include:

Plywood

OSB (Oriented Strand Board)

This layer supports the entire roofing system and distributes weight evenly across the structure.

Your decking:

Supports roofing materials

Provides structural stability

Holds roofing nails securely

Prevents sagging or uneven surfaces

Protects the attic from moisture intrusion

If decking becomes soft or damaged, shingles cannot properly anchor, leading to leaks and wind damage.

Both materials are widely used, but plywood is often preferred in areas with higher moisture exposure.

Decking may need replacement if:

It feels soft or spongy when walked on

There is visible rot or dark staining

Water damage has penetrated the wood

Nails do not hold properly

Sagging or uneven roof areas appear

During roof replacement, exposed decking is inspected carefully to determine if sections need repair or full replacement.

You might notice:

Ceiling stains or attic moisture

Roof sagging

Mold growth

Frequent leaks

Shingle buckling

Often, decking problems are hidden beneath roofing materials and only discovered during tear-off.

1️⃣ Old roofing materials are removed

2️⃣ Damaged decking sections are identified

3️⃣ Rotted plywood is cut out

4️⃣ New plywood sheets are installed

5️⃣ Proper fastening patterns are applied

6️⃣ Underlayment is installed over the new deck

Proper fastening and alignment are critical for structural integrity.

Most residential roofs use:

½-inch plywood (common standard)

⅝-inch plywood (for added strength)

Thickness depends on rafter spacing and local building requirements.

Improper decking installation can cause:

Shingle blow-offs

Nail pops

Uneven roof appearance

Early roof failure

Structural instability

The deck must be flat, solid, and securely fastened before underlayment is installed.

Decking should always be paired with:

Ice & water barrier at vulnerable areas

Synthetic underlayment

Proper attic ventilation

Ventilation prevents moisture buildup that can rot decking from the inside.

When protected properly:

Plywood decking can last 30+ years

Lifespan depends on ventilation and moisture exposure

Regular roof inspections help detect issues before major structural damage occurs.

Decking replacement cost depends on:

Amount of damaged area

Roof size

Accessibility

Material choice

Replacing small sections during roof replacement is far more affordable than repairing structural damage later.

Shingles are the visible layer, but decking is the structural backbone. A strong, properly installed plywood deck ensures your entire roofing system performs as designed.

If you’re planning a roof replacement, always make sure the decking is inspected and repaired as needed.
