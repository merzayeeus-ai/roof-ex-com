Gravel and Tar Roof Removal in San Francisco – Upgrade to a 3-Layer Modified Bitumen Torch-Down System

Gravel and tar roof removal in San Francisco is a critical upgrade for property owners dealing with aging built-up roofing systems. Many flat and low-slope buildings throughout San Francisco still have traditional gravel and tar (BUR) roofs that have reached the end of their service life. Due to constant coastal fog 🌫️, moisture exposure 🌊, UV rays ☀️, and temperature fluctuations, these roofs commonly develop leaks, ponding water, cracking, and membrane separation. Replacing an outdated gravel and tar roof with a modern 3-layer modified bitumen torch-down system significantly improves waterproofing performance, durability, and long-term protection while meeting California building code requirements. ✅

Complete Gravel and Tar Roof Tear-Off Process 🧹

The first step in a professional roof replacement in San Francisco is the complete removal of all gravel surfacing and built-up tar layers. Loose gravel is carefully removed, and the existing membrane is stripped down to the roof decking. A full structural inspection follows to identify damaged plywood, soft spots, or water intrusion. In San Francisco’s damp climate, replacing compromised decking is essential before installing the new roofing system. Proper preparation ensures maximum adhesion and long-lasting performance of the modified bitumen torch-down system. 🔍

3-Layer Modified Bitumen Torch-Down Installation 🔥

After the roof deck is prepared, a heavy-duty base sheet is installed as the first protective layer. The second layer consists of premium modified bitumen membrane applied using the torch-down method, creating a seamless waterproof barrier. The final granulated cap sheet adds UV resistance, weather protection, and durability. This 3-layer modified bitumen roofing system is ideal for flat roofs in San Francisco because it resists ponding water 💧, wind uplift 🌬️, and harsh Bay Area weather conditions while providing superior leak protection and long service life.

Long-Term Benefits for San Francisco Property Owners 🏠

Upgrading from gravel and tar roofing to a 3-layer modified bitumen torch-down system increases property value, reduces maintenance costs, and enhances overall structural protection. With proper drainage design and insulation, this system also improves energy efficiency and long-term reliability. For flat roof replacement in San Francisco, investing in a high-performance modified bitumen system ensures dependable, code-compliant results built to withstand Bay Area conditions. 🌉
