Roof Maintenance Tips for South San Francisco Homeowners

Proper roof maintenance extends your roof's lifespan by years and prevents costly emergency repairs. For South San Francisco homeowners, we recommend these seasonal practices: In fall, clean all gutters and downspouts before the rainy season, trim tree branches hanging within 6 feet of your roof, and inspect for damaged or missing shingles. In winter, check for leaks after every major storm and ensure downspouts are directing water away from your foundation. In spring, schedule a professional inspection to assess winter damage and address any issues before summer heat. In summer, check attic ventilation to prevent heat buildup that degrades shingles from below, and look for signs of moss or algae growth in shaded areas. Signs You Need a New Roof in South San Francisco

Knowing when to replace your roof saves you from escalating repair costs and potential interior damage. Key indicators that South San Francisco homeowners should watch for include: shingles that are curling, cracking, or losing granules (the sandpaper-like coating that protects the asphalt); daylight visible through the roof deck in your attic; sagging areas on the roof surface indicating structural deck damage; persistent leaks even after repairs; a roof age exceeding 20-25 years for asphalt shingles; dark streaks or moss growth covering large areas; and neighbors with similar-age homes already replacing their roofs.
